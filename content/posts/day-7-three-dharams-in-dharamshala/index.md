It had rained quite heavily last night. In fact, it was raining cats and dogs at 8:00 am. The rain subsided at around 11:00 am. I had a quick lunch (or brunch) and decided to cover the vicinity of McLeod Ganj on foot.

## A small walk to Bhaksunag waterfalls

Bhaksunag waterfall is a very short (2 km) walk from McLeod Ganj. The road passes through the Bhaksunag temple. The rains had worsened the junction that leads to the temple. It was still better than our Whitefield roads in Bangalore.

{{< figure src="DSC05054.jpg" caption="Bhaksunag temple is mostly maintained by the donations from the army. Most of it has historically come from the various Gurkha regiments stationed in Dharamshala." >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05055.jpg" caption="The inside of the temple has smaller rooms dedicated to various gods and saints" >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05057.jpg" caption="The courtyard of Bhaksunag temple has a swimming pool." >}}

The waterfall is 700 metres from the temple. The entire trail is laid out very nicely with slate. I saw a woman in high-heels walking through the paved trail. It was surely idiotic and dangerous, especially after the rains.

{{< figure src="DSC05063.jpg" caption="The entire trail is paved with local slate stones. There is also a railing for safety purposes." >}}

## H.H. Dalai Lama's temple and Tibetan Museum

While walking towards the temple, the entire place got covered in mist. Visibility was reduced to zero.

{{< figure src="DSC05072.jpg" caption="Even the parking platforms beyond the edge of the road aren't visible." >}}

The temple is dedicated to preserving the teachings of a particular form of Buddhism that can be collectively known as [Tibetan Tantric Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism). I got to know that specifically, there are four schools of thought, the details of which was difficult to grasp from the blurb on the [Kalachakra and Vishvamata idol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalachakra) inside the temple.

Sadly, photography is prohibited in most areas - some to preserve spiritual sanctity and the others for security reasons.

{{< figure src="DSC05074.jpg" caption="The residence of Dalai Lama is partially visible through the courtyard. Photography of his residence is prohibited due to security reasons." >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05080.jpg" caption="The Mani prayer wheels that surround the temple. Visitors are asked to move in clockwise direction." >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05082.jpg" caption="The Buddha statue of the temple. Some ancient scriptures are also preserved in this room." >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05085.jpg" caption="Monks lighting up butter lamps for offering." >}}

## St. John's Church in the Wilderness

The mist had cleared up at 4:00 pm. The Sun was visible through the clouds. I walked through the market and took NH 503, the longer of the two roads of that leads to Dharamshala.

{{< figure src="DSC05142.jpg" caption="The Sun was trying to break through the clouds." >}}

{{< figure src="DSC05143.jpg" caption="Sometimes you can't afford human mannequins. That's when Na'vi dolls come to the rescue." >}}

St. John's Church is indeed in the wilderness. There is nothing else nearby. It was built in 1852 to serve the British troops and families stationed in Dharamshala.

{{< figure src="DSC05150.jpg" caption="The Church is the only structure in Dharamshala that survived the 1905 Kangra earthquake. Parts of it have been rebuilt, including the spire and the roof." >}}
